How the night runs

Players are paired by rating so games stay competitive. Rapid time controls keep things moving. Between rounds, hang out and talk chess with whoever just beat you.

We'll usually set up a Swiss-style tournament through Chess.com each night to ensure multiple pairings and an opportunity to come out as the winner. If that's not your thing, feel free to come by and grab a couple games on the side. The purpose of Knights Out is not to be pressure packed — just good games and good company.

Why we started this

For people who love chess and want to improve with others.

We started Knights Out because we wanted a place to play chess with people who care about the game and want to have a good time meeting others while continuing to improve. A spot where you can learn a new opening, laugh about the game you just blundered away, and leave having made a connection with someone you genuinely want to play again.

The format is social by design. You'll play several rated games, get matched with people near your level, and have plenty of time between rounds to meet the group. Over time you'll start to recognize faces, track your progress, and build a rivalry or two.

Common questions

FAQ

Is my rating too low to come?+
Not at all. Everyone is welcome regardless of rating. You'll be paired with players near your level so games stay fun and competitive.
Do I need a chess.com account?+
Yes — we use your username and rating for pairings. It's free to sign up at chess.com and takes about two minutes.
Is this a tournament?+
We often do Swiss style tournaments to ensure clean matchups and consistent pairings, but this is a social chess night. No cost, no monetary prizes. Play hard, but the goal is to have fun and meet people.
What time format are games played in?+
Rapid — typically 10–15 minutes per side. Details confirmed closer to each event.
What does it cost?+
The event is free. Just show up.
How often do you meet?+
Once a month. Sign up above and we'll notify you when the next date is confirmed.
